How

In a very real sense, a project's outcome is determined before any keys are typed. Project costs (aka your effort) are determined by their initial design and construction; with poor planning your project will take 10x the effort to redesign during construction and several times that in post-release.

Minimizing this is an adequate goal for development. If you follow good practices your debugging cost is less, and if you maintain loose-coupling defensively, changes in design become changes in configuration.

I prefer command-line development, but ultimately use whatever is most expedient. For more details on technologies I've touched, check out my [stats] page.

While there are a plethora of buzzword-based approaches, I've found the most productivity in following iterative development approaches supported by test-cases.
